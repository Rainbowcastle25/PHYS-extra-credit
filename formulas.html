Formula Reference

Physics 1 Equation Sheet

Every formula organized by topic with plain-English descriptions. Hover any equation for a note.

Kinematics Motion
v = v₀ + atVelocity after time t at constant acceleration a
x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²Position as function of time (constant a)
v² = v₀² + 2aΔxVelocity–displacement relation, no time needed
Δx = ½(v + v₀)tDisplacement using average velocity
v̄ = Δx / ΔtAverage velocity definition
Range R = v₀² sin(2θ) / gProjectile range on flat ground
h_max = v₀ᵧ² / (2g)Maximum height of a projectile
Dynamics — Forces Newton's Laws
F_net = maNet force equals mass × acceleration (2nd Law)
w = mgWeight force (g = 9.8 m/s² near Earth)
f_k = μ_k NKinetic friction force (opposes motion)
f_s ≤ μ_s NStatic friction (maximum just before sliding)
F_spring = −kxHooke's law: spring force, k = spring constant
F_g = Gm₁m₂ / r²Universal gravitation (G = 6.674×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²)
Energy & Work Conservation
W = Fd cosθWork by constant force F over displacement d at angle θ
W_net = ΔKEWork–Energy Theorem
KE = ½mv²Kinetic energy of mass m moving at speed v
PE_g = mghGravitational potential energy (near surface)
PE_s = ½kx²Elastic potential energy, spring compressed/stretched by x
E_mech = KE + PE = const.Conservation of mechanical energy (no friction)
P = W/t = FvPower in watts (W = J/s)
Momentum & Impulse Collisions
p = mvLinear momentum (vector, kg·m/s)
J = FΔt = ΔpImpulse equals change in momentum
Σp_before = Σp_afterConservation of momentum (F_ext = 0)
v₁f = (m₁−m₂)v₁ᵢ/(m₁+m₂) + 2m₂v₂ᵢ/(m₁+m₂)Elastic collision final velocity for object 1
v_f = (m₁v₁ᵢ + m₂v₂ᵢ)/(m₁+m₂)Perfectly inelastic collision final velocity
x_cm = Σmᵢxᵢ / ΣmᵢCenter of mass position
Circular Motion Orbits
a_c = v²/r = ω²rCentripetal acceleration (toward center)
F_c = mv²/rCentripetal force (provided by existing forces)
v = 2πr/T = ωrTangential speed in circular motion
ω = 2π/T = 2πfAngular velocity (rad/s)
v_orbit = √(GM/r)Orbital speed for circular orbit
T² = (4π²/GM)r³Kepler's third law
Rotation & Torque Angular
s = rθ, v = rω, a = rαLinear–angular relationships
τ = rF sinθ = IαTorque; Newton's 2nd for rotation
I_disk = ½MR²Moment of inertia, solid disk about center
I_hoop = MR²Moment of inertia, hoop/ring about center
I_rod = ¹⁄₁₂ML² (center) or ⅓ML² (end)Uniform rod about center or end
I = I_cm + Md²Parallel-axis theorem (d = dist from cm to new axis)
L = IωAngular momentum; conserved when τ_net = 0
KE_rot = ½Iω²Rotational kinetic energy
KE_roll = ½mv² + ½Iω²Total KE of rolling object
Simple Harmonic Motion Oscillations
x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ)Position in SHM; A = amplitude, φ = phase
v(t) = −Aω sin(ωt + φ)Velocity in SHM; max at x = 0
a(t) = −Aω² cos(ωt + φ)Acceleration; max at x = ±A
ω = 2πf = 2π/TAngular frequency
T_spring = 2π√(m/k)Period of spring–mass system
T_pendulum = 2π√(L/g)Period of simple pendulum (small angle)
E_SHM = ½kA² = ½mv²_maxTotal energy in SHM; constant throughout
Gravitation Fields
F_g = Gm₁m₂/r²Universal law of gravitation; G = 6.674×10⁻¹¹
g = GM/r²Gravitational field strength at distance r from mass M
PE_g = −GMm/rGravitational potential energy (negative; zero at infinity)
v_esc = √(2GM/r)Escape velocity from surface of body with mass M, radius r
v_orbit = √(GM/r)Speed for circular orbit at radius r

Useful Constants

g = 9.8 m/s²Gravitational acceleration near Earth's surface
G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²Universal gravitational constant
M_Earth = 5.97 × 10²⁴ kgMass of Earth
R_Earth = 6.37 × 10⁶ mRadius of Earth
